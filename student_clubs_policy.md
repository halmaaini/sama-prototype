## Section 1: Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This policy governs the establishment, operation, governance, and dissolution of all student clubs/organizations at the University of Dubai (UD). It serves as the main reference document for both the Department of Student Services (DSS) staff, who are responsible for enforcing this policy, and all students who are expected to understand and comply with it.

The DSS Manager or his/her designated representative shall serve as the official supervisors to all student clubs. The DSS is responsible for safeguarding the well-being of student clubs and guiding their members in fulfilling their responsibilities.

### 1.2 Scope

This policy applies to all officially registered student clubs at the University of Dubai, their board members, and their general members.

### 1.3 Mission

The mission of the University of Dubai student clubs is to create an exciting and proactive student community by organizing and participating in events and activities related to their field of interest. The following objectives shall be pursued without discrimination on the grounds of age, sex, race, nationality, economic status, religion, creed, ability, or medical condition:

- To represent the interests of UD students and act as a channel of communication with the University and external bodies, through the DSS.
- To promote and encourage student participation in all UD clubs and activities.
- To increase awareness among new students about University of Dubai events and activities.
- To organize and promote club activities in collaboration with the UDSU and the DSS.
- To promote respect and appreciation for the local culture and traditions of the United Arab Emirates and to abide by its laws.
- To serve as a role model to UD students and help enforce UD ethical values, morals, rules, and policies.

## Section 2: Club Categories

All officially registered student clubs at the University of Dubai are classified under one of the following four categories:

| Category | Description & Examples |
|---|---|
| **Cultural Clubs** | Clubs that celebrate and promote the cultural heritage, traditions, and identity of specific nationalities or regions. Examples include: Emirati, Saudi, Palestinian, Syrian, Egyptian, Jordanian, Ethiopian, Afghan, Iranian, Indian, East African, Iraqi, Moroccan, Japanese, and Algerian clubs. |
| **Special Interest Clubs** | Clubs focused on shared interests, academic pursuits, or professional development. Examples include: Women's Club, Happiness Club, Debate Club, Gaming Club, and any similar club. |
| **Sports Clubs** | Clubs organized around sporting activities and athletic development. Examples include: Basketball, Volleyball, Football, etc. |
| **Community & Social Responsibility Clubs** | Clubs focused on volunteering, charity, and social impact. Examples include: Draw a Smile Club, which focuses on charitable activities such as supporting orphans, collecting and distributing meals for people in need, and similar community initiatives. |

## Section 3: Club Structure and Board

### 3.1 Board Composition

The executive authority of each club shall reside in its board. The board is comprised of three positions:

- President
- Vice-President (VP)
- Secretary

In addition to the board, each club must have a minimum of five (5) registered student members.

### 3.2 Board Eligibility Requirements

To hold any board position, a student must fulfill all of the following conditions:

- Be 18 years of age or older.
- Be enrolled in the current semester at UD.
- Have completed a minimum of twelve (12) credit hours at UD.
- Be committed to completing the next full academic year at UD.
- Have a minimum cumulative GPA of 2.0.
- Have a clear disciplinary record.
- Not holding any other student club board position simultaneously.

### 3.3 Roles and Responsibilities

#### President

- Act as the official representative of the club in all matters.
- Lead and run the club in accordance with this policy at all times.
- Chair all club-related meetings and activities.
- Submit a yearly activity report on behalf of the club to the DSS.
- Ensure the club organizes or participates in student activities and events throughout the academic year.
- Carry out any other duties arising during the year of office related to the presidential role.

#### Vice-President

- Assist and support the President in the day-to-day running of the club.
- Assume the role of President if the President is unable to fulfill his/her duties.
- Assist in dispute resolution within the club.
- Ensure all club members are fulfilling their assigned tasks.
- Ensure that the club's social media accounts are active, growing, and in compliance with UD rules and regulations.
- Oversee all promotional materials for activities and events.
- Liaise with the UDSU Events and Social Media Officers when support is needed.

#### Secretary

- Oversee and manage all financial matters related to the club.
- Prepare and submit the club's budget to DSS.
- Collect and maintain all financial records, expenses, and invoices.
- Ensure that all student records, timetables, and contact information are properly maintained and shared with the DSS.
- Prepare a yearly activity and financial report on behalf of the club to DSS and the incoming President.
- Be available to assist the club in activities beyond financial duties.

#### Members

- Attend and organize club activities and support the board in fulfilling the club's mission.
- Comply with this policy and the UD Code of Conduct at all times.

### 3.4 Club Advisor

Each club may optionally appoint a Club Advisor from the University of Dubai's academic or administrative staff. The appointment is initiated by the club and requires written confirmation of both the proposed Advisor and the DSS.

The Club Advisor serves in a purely advisory and supportive capacity for one academic year, renewable upon mutual agreement. The Advisor has no decision-making authority over the club or the DSS. The Advisor may:

- Provide guidance and mentorship to club board members.
- Offer academic or professional support relevant to the club's area of interest.
- Liaise between the club and the University where helpful.

The appointment of a Club Advisor does not affect the club's registration status or eligibility requirements.

## Section 4: Club Registration, Renewal, and Status

### 4.1 Club Registration (New Club)

Any student wishing to establish a new club may submit a Club Registration request to the DSS at any time during the academic year.

The Club Registration request must include:

- The proposed club name and category.
- The full name and Student ID of the President, Vice-President, and Secretary.
- A list of at least five (5) student members willing to join the club.

All applicants must meet the board eligibility requirements outlined in Section 3.2.

### 4.2 Annual Renewal Process

Club registration is not automatic. All clubs become Inactive at the end of the Spring semester each year. To remain or become Active for the following academic year, the club President must submit a Club Registration Form during the renewal window.

**Renewal Timeline:**

| Period | Action |
|---|---|
| End of Spring Semester / During Summer 1 | DSS sends an official communication to all active club Presidents inviting them to submit their Club Registration Form for the upcoming academic year. |
| End of Summer 1 | Deadline for renewal submissions. Clubs that have submitted a valid form are confirmed as Active with a known board structure (President, VP, Secretary). Clubs that have not submitted become Inactive. |
| Start of Fall Semester | DSS sends a second communication to all students announcing which clubs are Inactive, opening the opportunity for any student to reactivate them. |
| 2 Weeks After Fall Starts | Final picture is confirmed. All Active and Inactive clubs are officially communicated. Any remaining Inactive clubs may still be reactivated at any point during the academic year. |

If two Club Registration Forms are submitted by different groups for the same club name at the end of Summer 1, the DSS shall have the final authority to decide which team will lead the club for the upcoming academic year.

### 4.3 Club Status

Each club holds one of two official statuses at any given time:

- **Active** — The club has a confirmed board structure and has been formally approved by DSS for the current academic year.
- **Inactive** — The club has not completed the renewal process, has no confirmed President, or all board members have resigned or been removed. An Inactive club may be reactivated at any time by following the Club Registration process.

### 4.4 Reactivation of an Inactive Club

Any student wishing to reactivate an Inactive club must submit a Club Registration Form following the same process as a new club (Section 4.1). The club's name is reserved and cannot be claimed by another group while the club is Inactive.

### 4.5 DSS Clubs Register

The DSS shall maintain an official register of all student clubs, recording each club's name, category, status (Active/Inactive), members, and registration dates. This register is the authoritative source of record for all club-related matters.

## Section 5: Financial Management

### 5.1 Resource Allocation

To enable effective financial planning, the club board shall determine a set of aims and objectives for the club's activities for the coming academic year. The Secretary is responsible for all financial matters of the club, and the President shall exercise supervision over all finances.

The income of each club may comprise of:

- A variable percentage of annual endowment from UD, based on the student activity fee, upon application and subject to availability.
- Additional revenue raised by the club through approved fundraising or sponsorship activities.

The Secretary shall present audited accounts to the DSS. The accounts shall be audited by the University's Finance Department. A provisional budget should be submitted to the DSS before the end of the Spring Semester.

### 5.2 Event Proposal Form

An Event Proposal Form must be submitted to DSS for every club event, regardless of size or whether a budget is requested. No event may proceed without DSS review.

- If a budget is requested: the form must be submitted at least **3 weeks** before the event date.
- If no budget is requested: the form must be submitted at least **2 weeks** before the event date.

The Event Proposal Form must be prepared and submitted by the club President, Vice-President, or Secretary. DSS reserves the right to approve or decline any event proposal.

All receipts and financial documents related to an event must be submitted to DSS within **5 working days** after the event.

### 5.3 Purchasing Procedure

All club expenditures must follow the procedure imposed by UD Management:

1. The Purchase Request Form (PRF), along with quotes or invoices and the Event Proposal Form, shall be prepared and submitted by the Secretary to the DSS Representative at least three (3) weeks prior to the event.
2. The DSS follows standard university procedures to obtain the University of Dubai President's approval on all purchases.
3. Once approved, the DSS Representative deals directly with the club, which may then begin utilizing the allocated funds.

### 5.4 Promotion, Selling, Sponsorship and Fundraising

Any use of UD facilities for advertisement or selling of external organizations' products, services, or programs requires prior DSS approval. To bring an external contributor, the club must first approach the DSS.

Similarly, any external sponsorship or fundraising activity, whether inside or outside the UD campus, requires prior DSS approval before the club engages with any sponsor or fundraising partner.

All income secured through promotion, selling, sponsorship, or fundraising must be formalized through a contract or agreement, submitted to and approved by the DSS. All external contributors' bank cheques must be made payable to the University of Dubai.

All expenditures shall be authorized by the President or Vice-President of the club, the Head of DSS, and the UD Finance Manager, and shall be duly recorded. All funds must be spent for the benefit of the student body.

## Section 6: Club Activities

### 6.1 Events and Activities

Student clubs are encouraged to organize or participate in at least two (2) events or activities per semester. These may include events organized independently by the club or in collaboration with the UDSU, DSS, or other clubs.

All events must be submitted through the Event Proposal Form process outlined in Section 5.2 prior to taking place.

### 6.2 Annual Report

Each club President is required to submit an annual report to DSS at the end of the academic year, summarizing the club's activities, events, financial summary, and achievements for that year. The Secretary shall assist in preparing this report.

## Section 7: Resignation and Removal

### 7.1 Resignation

The resignation process depends on the position of the resigning member:

- A **regular member** who wishes to resign must inform the club President. No DSS involvement is required.
- The **Vice-President or Secretary** who wishes to resign must inform the President, who is then responsible for notifying the DSS in writing.

### 7.2 Removal

The removal process depends on the position of the member concerned:

- A **regular member** may be removed from the club by a joint decision of the President, Vice-President, and Secretary. DSS involvement is not required.
- The **President, Vice-President, or Secretary** may be removed from their position by the DSS, which holds the final decision-making authority in such cases.

Grounds for automatic removal from any board position include:

- Failure to maintain a minimum cumulative GPA of 2.0.
- A breach of the UD Code of Conduct resulting in disciplinary sanctions.

Only the DSS is entitled to remove an elected or nominated board member as deemed necessary or to serve the best interests of the student body.

---

## Section 8: Vacancies

In the event of a vacancy in any board position, the following rules apply:

### 8.1 President Vacancy

If the President's position becomes vacant for any reason, the Vice-President automatically assumes the role of President. No DSS approval is required for this transition.

### 8.2 Vice-President or Secretary Vacancy

If the Vice-President or Secretary's position becomes vacant, the remaining board members shall organize a selection process among eligible students and appoint a replacement by majority decision. DSS shall be notified of the appointment.

### 8.3 Full Board Vacancy

If all three board positions (President, Vice-President, and Secretary) become vacant simultaneously, the club automatically becomes Inactive. Any student wishing to reactivate the club must follow the Club Registration process outlined in Section 4.

## Section 9: Board Acknowledgment

Prior to assuming their position, all club Presidents must sign a collective acknowledgment document maintained by the DSS. By signing, each President confirms that:

- They have read, understood, and agree to comply with this Student Clubs Policy in its entirety.
- They are responsible for the effective and compliant operation of their club.
- They will ensure that all board members and general members of their club are made aware of and comply with this policy.
- They understand that any misconduct or breach of this policy may result in sanctions, up to and including removal from their position.

This acknowledgment document shall be signed at the beginning of each academic year, upon club registration or renewal, and maintained on file by the DSS.

## Section 10: Complaint Procedure

Any student who is dissatisfied with a club or believes they have been unfairly treated by a club or its members may submit a written complaint to the DSS Representative.

A decision shall be made within **four (4) working days** of receipt of the written complaint.

## Section 11: Office Use Guidelines

The University of Dubai provides a shared office space for student club board members and Student Union (UDSU) members to fulfill their duties. All students using this office must observe the following guidelines at all times:

- Abide by the UD Code of Conduct at all times.
- The office is shared with UDSU members and operates from **8:30 AM to 9:00 PM** during weekdays, except on Fridays and public holidays.
- Except for the UDSU President and Vice-President, no desks are permanently assigned. Only specific storage spaces are allocated to each club.
- The office is exclusively for club board members, UDSU members, and students temporarily involved in club or UDSU events.
- A professional working environment must be maintained at all times.
- The University is not responsible for any loss or damage to personal belongings. No personal items should be left unattended in the office.
- Desks must be kept clean and tidy at all times, as they are shared among multiple students.
- Food is not permitted inside the office.
- Writing, painting, or sticking papers and posters on walls, window glass, desks, chairs, or any other property is strictly prohibited.
- Office computers and phones are to be used for official business purposes only.
- Email credentials are personal and must not be shared with anyone. Each user is accountable for all content sent from their email account.
- Computers must be turned off after use.

Breach of any of these guidelines will result in referral to UD management for a disciplinary investigation. Penalties range from a verbal warning to dismissal from the university, depending on the severity of the misconduct.
