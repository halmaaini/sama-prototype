## Section 1: Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This policy governs the structure, election, operation, governance, and accountability of the University of Dubai Students Union (UDSU). It serves as the reference document for both the Department of Student Services (DSS) staff, who are responsible for enforcing this policy, and all UDSU members and students, who are expected to understand and comply with it.

The DSS Manager or his/her designated representative shall serve as the official supervisor to the UDSU. The DSS is responsible for safeguarding the well-being of the UDSU and guiding its members in fulfilling their responsibilities.

### 1.2 Scope

This policy applies to all elected and nominated members of the UDSU Cabinet, as well as all University of Dubai students in their interactions with the UDSU.

### 1.3 Mission

The mission of the University of Dubai Students' Union is to create an exciting and proactive student community by:

- Encouraging students to take responsibility for handling their own matters and having a say in the decision-making process at the university level.
- Advocating the needs and interests of undergraduate students.
- Promoting learning outside the classroom and developing and implementing both innovative and traditional programs.
- Organizing and participating in local and international events.

### 1.4 Aims and Objectives

The following aims and objectives shall be pursued without discrimination on the grounds of age, sex, race, nationality, economic status, religion, creed, ability, or medical condition:

- To be the official, unbiased, and impartial representative body for all students attending the University of Dubai.
- To advance the education of students at the University as a whole.
- To represent the interests of UD students and act as a channel of communication with the University and external bodies, through the DSS.
- To promote and protect the welfare of all UD students.
- To defend students' rights and represent their views in front of UD management.
- To promote and encourage student participation in the Students' Union, clubs, organizations, sports, and all other UD activities.
- To provide opportunities for student engagement and personal development.
- To explore students' talents, capacities, and skills, and to nurture their creativity.
- To increase awareness among new students about the University of Dubai.
- To assist in organizing and promoting club activities in collaboration with the DSS.
- To promote respect and appreciation for the local culture and traditions of the United Arab Emirates and to abide by its laws.
- To serve as a role model to UD students and help enforce UD rules and policies while promoting strong ethics and morals.

### 1.5 Encouragements

In addition to their core responsibilities, the UDSU is encouraged to:

- Establish internal rules and policies for the UDSU, in coordination with and subject to DSS approval.
- Coordinate and foster collaboration with student unions at other universities or higher education institutions.

### 1.6 Out of Scope

This policy and the authority of the UDSU do not extend to the following:

- The UDSU represents the student body as a whole and is not responsible for addressing personal or individual student issues. Students with personal concerns are encouraged to contact the Department of Student Services directly.
- The UDSU does not have authority over academic matters, including but not limited to grading, curriculum design, academic appeals, or faculty-related decisions, which remain under the jurisdiction of UD academic bodies.
- The governance and internal operations of individual student clubs fall outside the direct authority of the UDSU, although the UDSU may collaborate with and support student clubs in coordination with the DSS.
- The UDSU is not authorized to engage in political activity or profit-driven commercial activities on behalf of any external party.
- This policy does not apply to individuals who are not currently enrolled at the University of Dubai.

## Section 2: Cabinet Structure

The executive authority of the UDSU shall reside with the Cabinet. The purpose of the Cabinet is to serve UD students as initiators and leaders, operating as a team to ensure efficiency, transparency, competence, and accountability.

The Cabinet is composed of three distinct groups:

| Group | Positions | How Selected |
|---|---|---|
| **Top 4** | President, Vice-President, Treasurer, Secretary | University-wide election by all UD students as a single ticket |
| **College Representatives** | One representative per college (Dubai Business School, College of Engineering & IT, College of Law) | Self-nomination, interviewed by Top 4, suggested to DSS, approved by DSS and Dean of each college |
| **Officers** | Events Officers and Media Officers | Self-nomination, interviewed by Top 4, suggested to DSS, approved by DSS |

The Cabinet may delegate specific responsibilities to individual students or sub-committees as deemed necessary.

The University of Dubai may provide an office to the UDSU Cabinet members on the UD premises. This office must abide by UD policy, rules, and regulations as outlined in Section 11 of this policy.

## Section 3: Elections

### 3.1 General Elections

The DSS shall conduct the general election of the Top 4 (President, Vice-President, Treasurer, and Secretary) at the end of the Spring semester, or at the latest at the beginning of the Fall semester.

The Top 4 shall run as a single, non-splitting ticket. If any one member of the team does not meet the eligibility requirements, the entire team is considered invalid and may not participate in the election.

Once elected, the Cabinet members shall take office and begin their duties at the start of the next academic year.

### 3.2 Qualification Requirements

**President:**

- Must be enrolled in the current semester at UD.
- Have a minimum cumulative GPA of 2.8.
- Have clear disciplinary and academic standing records.
- Have completed at least twenty-one (21) credit hours and must complete the next full academic year at UD.
- Must attach a list of ten (10) students supporting his/her nomination.

**Vice-President, Secretary, and Treasurer:**

- Must be enrolled in the current semester at UD.
- Have a minimum cumulative GPA of 2.5.
- Have clear disciplinary and academic standing records.
- Have completed at least twenty-one (21) credit hours and must complete the next full academic year at UD.

**College Representatives and Officers:**

- Must be enrolled in the current semester at UD.
- Have a minimum cumulative GPA of 2.0.
- Have clear disciplinary and academic standing records.
- Have completed at least twenty-one (21) credit hours and must complete the next full academic year at UD.

### 3.3 Selection of College Representatives and Officers

Following the election of the Top 4, the DSS shall send an official communication to all students inviting applications for College Representative, Events Officer, and Media Officer positions.

The selection process is as follows:

1. Interested students self-nominate by submitting an official application form within the stated deadline.
2. The Top 4 conducts interviews with all eligible applicants.
3. The Top 4 selects their preferred candidates and submits their suggestions to DSS.
4. DSS reviews and gives final approval. For College Representatives, the Dean of the relevant college must also approve.

### 3.4 Before Elections

The DSS Representative overseeing the elections shall:

- Prepare forms and coordinate the confirmation of candidate applications, including student name, ID number, and desired position.
- Ensure all candidates meet and maintain the eligibility requirements.
- Post the date and time of the elections.
- Facilitate an orientation session for all Top 4 candidates to familiarize them with the election process and timeline.

### 3.5 Campaigning

All candidates shall adhere to the following campaign procedures:

- Campaign materials must be approved and stamped by the DSS prior to use.
- The DSS must approve the placement of campaign materials inside any campus building.
- Campaigning begins following the candidate orientation session, at a time specified by the DSS.
- All campaign materials must be removed by candidates immediately following the elections.

### 3.6 Voting

- Voting in UDSU elections shall be organized online, without excluding the possibility of regular or offline voting in future elections.
- Every registered UD student has the opportunity to vote for the Top 4 as a single ticket.
- The elections shall be the responsibility of the DSS, which will inform all students by email about the election process and timeline.

### 3.7 Election Results

- Votes will be counted and announced immediately following the closing of the elections by the DSS Representative.
- The team that receives the highest number of votes shall be elected. In the case of a tie, a run-off election shall be held by the DSS to break the tie.
- Results will be final unless the DSS receives a written appeal within two (2) working days of the date of voting.
- The DSS shall announce the official results to all UD students.
- In the case where only one team is running, that team wins by default. The orientation shall be conducted, but the vote and campaign will be cancelled.

### 3.8 Appeal on Election Results

Once declared by the DSS Representative, election results shall be considered valid unless a written appeal is filed within two (2) working days of the voting date. Any appeal must clearly state the grounds for the complaint with proper supporting evidence. The DSS will announce its decision within five (5) working days.

### 3.9 Election Penalties

Any failure to abide by the rules of this policy during elections will result in penalties imposed by UD management:

- Any candidate or person who uses an ID that is not their own when voting shall be immediately expelled from the election.
- Any candidate who attempts to tamper with any voting mechanism will be sanctioned.
- Penalties range from a verbal warning to expulsion from UD, based on the severity of the case.
- Any candidate or team wishing to appeal a penalty must do so in writing within twenty-four (24) hours. The DSS will announce its decision within ten (10) college days.

### 3.10 Anticipated Elections

An anticipated election will only take place in the case where the elected President, Vice-President, Treasurer, and/or Secretary resign or are removed. Anticipated elections follow the exact same process as regular elections.

## Section 4: Roles and Responsibilities

The following describes the duties and responsibilities of each UDSU Cabinet position. Financial and administrative tasks related to specific events may be carried out by any Cabinet member involved in organizing that event, regardless of their official title.

### 4.1 President

- Act as the elected President in the promotion, development, and effective operation of the UDSU.
- Run the UDSU in accordance with this policy and all relevant UD legislation at all times.
- Represent the student body and the UDSU on key University of Dubai boards and committees.
- Strive to achieve maximum representation of all UD undergraduate students at individual, university, and national level.
- Provide leadership, promote unity and teamwork, and coordinate the effectiveness of the Cabinet.
- Attend and chair all UDSU Cabinet meetings.
- Be available on campus to meet students when needed.
- Carry out any other duties that may arise during the year of office related to the Presidential role.

### 4.2 Vice-President

- Assist and support the President in the running of the UDSU.
- Assume the role of President if the President is unable to fulfill his/her duties.
- Represent all students at events and committees to the best of their ability.
- Assist the President in dispute resolution within any organizations.
- Ensure all Cabinet members are fulfilling their tasks.
- Provide leadership and support to all UDSU members and student representatives.
- Be available on campus to meet students when needed.

### 4.3 Treasurer

- Support the oversight of financial matters related to UDSU activities and events.
- Assist in the preparation of event budgets and related financial documentation where applicable.
- Be available to assist the UDSU in activities beyond financial duties.

### 4.4 Secretary

- Ensure that records, timetables, and contact information, including all students' involvement, are stored appropriately and shared with the DSS.
- Ensure UDSU meetings are properly scheduled and organized.
- Be responsible for keeping and distributing accurate minutes of Cabinet meetings.
- Distribute contact lists to Cabinet members.
- Be available to assist the UDSU in activities beyond administrative duties.

### 4.5 College Representatives

- Listen to student concerns or ideas from their college programs and bring those concerns to UDSU Cabinet meetings.
- Ensure the general welfare of students from the college they represent.
- Participate in regular meetings with college faculty representatives.

### 4.6 Events Officers

- Lead the planning and delivery of the UDSU events program.
- Ensure UDSU events are in line with UDSU policies and procedures.
- Produce event plans, budgets, running orders, theme concepts, scripts, and key contact lists.
- Conduct venue visits where applicable.
- Liaise with suppliers, contractors, and the purchasing department through the DSS.
- Ensure all events have a positive impact on the student's experience.
- Work with the Media Officers to decide on marketing plans, concepts, and designs.
- Attend and support event debrief meetings.
- Help with DSS and student club events when needed.
- Act reasonably and prudently in all matters and in the best interest of the UDSU.

### 4.7 Media Officers

- Ensure that the UDSU social media accounts are active and followed by the maximum number of UD students.
- Work on creating an active role for student media in promoting the UDSU mission, values, services, and campaigns.
- Be in charge of creating all posters and designs required by the UDSU and student clubs.
- Develop and lead the creative team that produces content for the UDSU and student organizations.
- Liaise with Events Officers in the delivery of UDSU and club events.
- Develop opportunities and training for students to volunteer with student media.
- Constantly explore new ways of delivering media and information to UD students.
- Act reasonably and prudently in all matters and in the best interest of the UDSU.

## Section 5: Financial Management

### 5.1 Budget Management

The DSS holds and manages the budget allocated to the UDSU for all events and activities. UDSU members do not manage funds directly. Any Cabinet member involved in organizing an event may submit the Event Proposal Form to DSS, regardless of their official title.

### 5.2 Event Proposal Form

An Event Proposal Form must be submitted to DSS for every UDSU event, regardless of size or whether a budget is requested. No event may proceed without DSS review and approval.

- If a budget is requested: the form must be submitted at least 3 weeks before the event date.
- If no budget is requested: the form must be submitted at least 2 weeks before the event date.

DSS reserves the right to approve or decline any event proposal. All receipts and financial documents related to an event must be submitted to DSS within 5 working days after the event.

### 5.3 Promotion, Selling, Sponsorship and Fundraising

Any use of UD facilities for advertisement or selling of external organizations' products, services, or programs requires prior DSS approval. To bring an external contributor, the UDSU must first approach the DSS.

Any external sponsorship or fundraising activity — whether inside or outside the UD campus — requires prior DSS approval before the UDSU engages with any sponsor or fundraising partner.

All income secured through promotion, selling, sponsorship, or fundraising must be formalized through a contract or agreement, submitted to and approved by the DSS. All external contributors' bank cheques must be made payable to the University of Dubai with "UDSU" in the description.

All expenditures shall be authorized by the President or Vice-President of the UDSU, the Manager of the DSS, and the UD Finance Manager, and shall be duly recorded. All funds must be spent for the benefit of the student body.

## Section 6: UDSU Cabinet Meetings

To discuss past or upcoming issues, actions, or events, the UDSU Cabinet must organize meetings at least twice per month and share the meeting minutes with the DSS.

The matters of UDSU meetings shall be taken in the following order:

1. Approval of Minutes of Meeting
2. Previous action points
3. Matters arising
4. College matters
5. Student issues

The President of the UDSU shall chair all meetings. If he/she is absent, resigned, or released from his/her position, the chair shall be taken by the Vice-President, followed by the Secretary, then a representative of the DSS.

Each Cabinet member has one vote. In the event of a tie, the chairperson shall have the deciding vote.

No vote shall be recorded on behalf of an absent member.

In the event of any situation not covered in the meeting agenda, the Chairperson shall rule on the procedure to be adopted.

All actions arising shall be recorded during meetings and assigned to one member, along with a date by which the action should be executed. The Secretary oversees UDSU meetings and will send out the agenda and minutes to all Cabinet members. If the Secretary's position is vacant, the President will be in charge of informing them.

## Section 7: Resignation and Removal

### 7.1 Resignation

Any Cabinet member who wishes to resign must submit a written resignation to the UDSU Secretary, who will inform the DSS.

### 7.2 Removal

The removal process depends on the position of the member concerned:

- Any Cabinet member other than the President may be removed through a decision made internally by the Top 4. However, DSS approval must be obtained before the removal is considered official.
- The UDSU President may only be removed by the DSS. The President of UD holds the final decision in such cases.

Grounds for automatic removal from any Cabinet position include:

- Failure to maintain the minimum cumulative GPA required for their position.
- A breach of the UD Code of Conduct or the UDSU Code of Conduct resulting in disciplinary sanctions.
- Failure to maintain a clear disciplinary standing.

---

## Section 8: Vacancies

In the event of a vacancy in any Cabinet position, the DSS shall send an official communication to all students informing them of the situation. The following rules then apply depending on the position:

### 8.1 President Vacancy

If the President's position becomes vacant for any reason, the Vice-President automatically assumes the role of President. The Top 4 becomes a Top 3, with the Secretary and Treasurer retaining their existing positions. No replacement is sought for the Vice-President position in this scenario.

### 8.2 Other Cabinet Vacancies

If any other Cabinet position becomes vacant (Vice-President after succession, Treasurer, Secretary, Events Officer, or Media Officer), the following process applies:

1. DSS sends an official communication to all students announcing the vacancy.
2. Interested students submit an application.
3. The Top 4 conducts interviews with eligible applicants.
4. The Top 4 selects their preferred candidate and submits their suggestion to DSS.
5. DSS gives final approval and the new member is appointed.

### 8.3 College Representative Vacancy

If a College Representative's position becomes vacant, the following process applies:

1. DSS sends an official communication to all students announcing the vacancy.
2. Interested students submit an application.
3. The Top 4 conducts interviews with eligible applicants.
4. The Top 4 suggests their preferred candidate to DSS.
5. Both DSS and the Dean of the relevant college must approve the appointment.

### 8.4 Full Top 4 Vacancy

If all four members of the Top 4 (President, Vice-President, Treasurer, and Secretary) resign or are removed simultaneously, the DSS shall temporarily lead the UDSU until anticipated elections are organized and completed in accordance with Section 3.

- Any confidential discussions or documents they encounter in their role will not be shared with any external party outside of the UDSU Cabinet members and DSS staff.

## Section 10: Complaint Procedure

Any student who is dissatisfied with their interactions or experience with the UDSU, or who believes they have been unfairly treated, may submit a written complaint to the DSS Representative.

A decision shall be communicated to the complainant within four (4) working days of receipt of the written complaint.

## Section 11: Office Use Guidelines

The University of Dubai provides a shared office space for UDSU Cabinet members and student club board members to fulfill their duties. All students using this office must observe the following guidelines at all times:

- Abide by the UD Code of Conduct at all times.
- The office is shared with student club members and operates from 8:30 AM to 9:00 PM during weekdays, except on Fridays and public holidays.
- Except for the UDSU President and Vice-President, no desks are permanently assigned. Only specific storage space is allocated to the UDSU and each club.
- The office is exclusively for UDSU Cabinet members, club board members, and students temporarily involved in UDSU or club events.
- A professional working environment must be maintained at all times.
- The University is not responsible for any loss or damage to personal belongings. No personal items should be left unattended in the office.
- Desks must be kept clean and tidy at all times, as they are shared among multiple students.
- Food is not permitted inside the office.
- Writing, painting, or sticking papers and posters on walls, window glass, desks, chairs, or any other property is strictly prohibited.
- Office computers and phones are to be used for official business purposes only.
- Email credentials are personal and must not be shared with anyone. Each user is accountable for all content sent from their email account.
- Computers must be turned off after use.

Breach of any of these guidelines will result in referral to UD management for a disciplinary investigation. Penalties range from a verbal warning to dismissal from the university, depending on the severity of the misconduct.

## 3.4 Club Advisor

Each club may optionally appoint a Club Advisor from the University of Dubai's academic or administrative staff. The appointment is initiated by the club and requires the written confirmation of both the proposed Advisor and the DSS.

The Club Advisor serves in a purely advisory and supportive capacity for one academic year, renewable upon mutual agreement. The Advisor has no decision-making authority over the club or the DSS. The Advisor may:

- Provide guidance and mentorship to club board members.
- Offer academic or professional support relevant to the club's area of interest.
- Liaise between the club and the University where helpful.

The appointment of a Club Advisor does not affect the club's registration status or eligibility requirements.
